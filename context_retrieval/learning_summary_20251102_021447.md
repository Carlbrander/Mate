## Facts & Concepts Learned

**Basic Biology:**
- Penguins are flightless, semi-aquatic seabirds living almost exclusively in the Southern Hemisphere
- They possess countershaded plumage, flippers for swimming, spiny tongues, and powerful jaws
- Penguins spend approximately half their lives on land and half in the ocean

**Species & Size Diversity:**
- **Largest:** Emperor penguin - 1.1 meters tall, weighing 35 kilograms
- **Smallest:** Little blue (fairy) penguin - 30-33 cm tall, weighing 1.2-1.3 kilograms
- One species (Gal√°pagos penguin) lives at the equator, breaking the "cold climate only" assumption

**Diet & Feeding:**
- Primary diet consists of krill, fish, squid, and other marine life
- Specialized anatomical features aid in catching and consuming prey

**Evolutionary Context:**
- Penguins have existed for 62 million years
- Some prehistoric penguin species were as large as adult humans

**Habitat & Conservation:**
- Most species inhabit cold regions, particularly Antarctica
- Various species like Adelie, chinstrap, gentoo, and African penguins occupy different ecological niches
